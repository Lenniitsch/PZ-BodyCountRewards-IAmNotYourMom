description=[h2]FAQ[/h2]
description=
description=[b]Q: Why are these traits not in the base BCR mod?[/b]
description=A: The base mod aims for lore-friendly, balanced selections. This addon is for players who want anything goes. I am not your mom. You decide.
description=
description=[b]Q: I keep getting Brave but I want Deaf. What gives?[/b]
description=A: Deaf costs 12 points (Very Rare), Brave costs 4 (Uncommon). Weighted random selection means cheaper traits drop more often. Deaf will show up eventually as you earn more milestones and the cheaper traits get cleared from the pool. You cannot target a specific trait directly.
description=
description=[b]Q: Can other mods add traits like this?[/b]
description=A: Yes. BCR has a public extensibility API. This addon doubles as a reference implementation. The entire thing is only about 147 lines of Lua. See the For Mod Developers section below.
description=
description=[b]Q: Removed Short Sighted but still can't see far?[/b]
description=A: The trait is removed correctly from your character, but the visual effect (blur/vision range) does not refresh until you reload the game (quit to main menu and load your save). This is a PZ engine limitation, not a BCR bug.
description=
description=[h2]For Mod Developers[/h2]
description=[b]Download this mod and study the source code.[/b] It is the simplest possible example of integrating custom traits into Body Count Rewards:
description=[list]
description=[*] Call [b]BCR.RegisterCustomTraits()[/b] with your trait data, exclusions, and sandbox namespace
description=[*] Traits merge automatically into BCR's reward pools, UI catalog, and history
description=[*] Full self-test included. Run [b]BCRIAmNotYourMom_RunTests()[/b] in the console to see how validation works.
description=[*] BCR core also provides [b]BCR.RunThirdPartyTests()[/b] to verify all addons at once.
description=[/list]
